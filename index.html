Introduction




An intelligent system is a technologically advanced computer-based system that can represent, reason about, and interpret data. In doing so it can learn about the structure of the data, analyse the data to extract patterns and meaning, derive new information, and identify strategies and behaviours to act on the results of its analysis.

Intelligent systems come in many forms and have many applications, from processing huge data sets to controlling robots and drones. The ideas and concepts are drawn from the areas of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and a range of fields such as psychology, linguistics and brain sciences, forming many interdisciplinary relationships.

History

How it all began...

  • 1955 - 1956

    The Birth of Artificial Intelligence

    John McCarthy coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" in his 1955 proposal for the 1956 Dartmouth Artificial Intelligence conference, which gave birth to the field.

  • 1956 – 1974

    The Golden Age

    During this period, people were amazed that computers were able to accomplish impressive tasks like solving problems and proving theorems in geometry and algebra.
    This period also marked the completion of the world's first full-scale anthropomorphic robot built in the world, WABOT-1.

  • 1974 - 1980

    The First AI Winter

    The field had little progress due to limited computing power and the lack of data.
    The constraint in computing power led to tremendous amount of time required to find optimal solutions to many problems. This also meant that many AI solutions could never be scaled up into useful systems.
    The lack of data was an impeding factor to the progress of vision and natural language AI applications, which required a vast amount of information.

  • 1980 - 1987

    The AI Boom

    Expert system, a form of AI program that attempts to act like a human expert on a particular subject area, began to be widely adopted by organisations around the world.
    The popularisation of a form of neural network (Hopfield network) and a new method for training them (backpropagation) led to the progress in optical character and speech recognition.

  • 1987 - 1993

    The Second AI Winter

    With the rise of the microcomputers, desktop computers became more powerful than the more expensive Lisp machines that used specialised AI hardware. This led to the sudden collapse of a half a billion dollars industry, and the cutting of research funds.

  • 1993 - 2000

    The Rise of AI

    Algorithms developed by AI researchers started to integrate into larger systems and industrial processes such as data mining, medical diagnosis and Google's search engine.

  • 2000 – Present

    Big Data & Deep Learning

    Access to large amount of data and advanced machine learning techniques, paired with greater computing power, led to the successful progress and research of various applications and problems.
